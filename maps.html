Static Maps

Static maps will always be relevant. Static maps should communicate a clear story, the cartographer should do the work for the reader. A static map should be easy to read quickly. Did you know you can make static maps with Microsoft Excel 2016? I make static maps mostly using QGIS.

The Modifiable Aerial Unit Problem is illustrated in this interactive example showing the Sustainable Development Goal Indicator 15.1.1 Forest area as a proportion of the total land area reported in the year 2000 by country and by regions. What patterns change based on the aerial unit?

Big Data

Above is an example displaying elevation in the Netherlands. Red is 6 meters high while dark blue is 1 meter above sea level. Derived from LiDAR imagery. Remote Sensing has always and will always produce big data. Google Earth Engine is a tool for big spatial data wrangling. I have lead several student projects related to monitoring change over time-related to carbon emission, carbon sequestration, forest cover change, and more. Remotely sensed satellite imagery is free, open-source, big data with countless research opportunities limited only by our imagination.
